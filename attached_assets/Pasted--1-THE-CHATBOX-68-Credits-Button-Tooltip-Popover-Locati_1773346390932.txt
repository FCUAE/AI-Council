### 4. FAQ PLACEMENT AND CONTENT — Under the Packages, Not Above

**Decision: The user is right. This belongs under the packages as a proper FAQ section. But also keep ONE pre-price objection callout above the tiers.**

Here's why both placements serve different purposes:

**Above the tier cards (1 single line, not an accordion):**
This isn't an FAQ. It's a conversion intervention. One sentence that neutralizes the "why is this more expensive than ChatGPT" reaction before they see numbers:

> **Why does this cost more than a regular AI chat?** Because every session runs your question through 4 models and 3 rounds of debate. You're not paying for one answer. You're paying for the process to get the most intelligent answer.

No accordion. No expand/collapse. Just a clean line of text with a subtle background. This is objection-handling, not information architecture.

**Below the tier cards (full FAQ accordion, 6 questions):**

**Q1: Why does one debate use multiple credits?**
> Each debate isn't one AI call. It's 12+ API calls across 4 models, with multiple rounds where they challenge each other. The credits reflect the actual compute behind every answer you get.

**Q2: Is this worth it for every question?**
> No, and we'd rather be honest about that. If you need a quick fact or a simple rewrite, a regular AI chat is fine. The Council is for the questions where a shallow answer could actually cost you: a business decision, a strategy call, a complex problem where blind spots matter.

**Q3: What happens to my credits if I don't use them?**
> Credits are active for 60 days from purchase. We'll remind you 30 days before so nothing goes to waste.

**Q4: Can I pick which AI models are in the debate?**
> Yes. You can choose your model lineup before each debate. Premium thinking models use more credits per session but go deeper.

**Q5: How is this different from asking the same question to 4 AI tools myself?**
> Three things you can't do manually: (1) The models respond to each other's arguments, not just your original question. (2) There's a structured challenge round where weak reasoning gets exposed. (3) A final synthesis forces them to either agree or clearly flag where they disagree. Copying your question into 4 tabs gives you 4 separate answers. This gives you one answer that 4 models fought over.

**Q6: Will you offer a monthly subscription?**
> We're working on it. Right now, credit packs with a 60-day window let you try the product without a recurring commitment. When subscriptions launch, existing users will get first access.
